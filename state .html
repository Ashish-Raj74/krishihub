1. Bihar Hari Khad Yojana

This scheme provides financial compensation to farmers whose crops have been damaged due to natural disasters such as floods, droughts, hailstorms, or cyclones. It aims to support affected farmers by offering quick monetary assistance so they can recover from losses and continue their agricultural activities without severe financial burden. The scheme is managed by the Bihar government and requires proper documentation and verification to claim the benefit.

Watch Video Official Link

2. Bihar Diesel Grant Scheme

Under this scheme, the Bihar government provides a diesel subsidy to farmers to support irrigation needs, especially during insufficient rainfall or delayed monsoon. The grant is given based on landholding and is credited directly to the farmer's bank account. The scheme helps in reducing operational costs during critical farming stages and encourages uninterrupted cultivation despite climatic irregularities.

Watch Video Official Link

3. Bihar Rajya Fasal Sahayata Yojana

This is a crop assistance scheme launched by the Bihar government as an alternative to traditional crop insurance. Instead of depending on insurance companies, the government directly provides financial help to farmers whose crops are damaged due to unforeseen natural events. The payout ranges from ₹7,500 to ₹10,000 per hectare depending on the extent of damage. It ensures timely and fair compensation without premium payments.

Watch Video Official Link

4. Bihar Saur Kranti Sinchai Yojana

This solar irrigation scheme offers up to 75% subsidy to farmers for purchasing solar-powered pump sets. The aim is to reduce dependency on diesel and electricity for irrigation, making farming more cost-effective and eco-friendly. It is especially useful for areas with limited power supply and helps promote green energy usage in rural agricultural practices.

Watch Video Official Link

5. Krishi Input Anudan Yojana

This scheme provides financial support for the purchase of agricultural inputs such as seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, and soil nutrients to farmers affected by natural calamities. The assistance ensures that farmers do not abandon cultivation due to input costs after a disaster. The scheme plays a key role in promoting post-disaster recovery and continued food production.

Watch Video Official Link

6. Mukhyamantri Tivra Beej Vistar Yojana

This scheme offers up to 90% subsidy on certified and high-yielding seed varieties to farmers across Bihar. It aims to increase crop productivity, reduce dependency on low-quality seeds, and promote modern agricultural practices. The seeds provided under this scheme are tested for better germination and resistance, improving both quantity and quality of harvest.

Watch Video Official Link

7. Bihar Agri Investment Promotion Policy (BAIPP)

BAIPP provides financial incentives and capital subsidies to farmers and entrepreneurs looking to set up agricultural infrastructure such as cold storages, food processing units, packaging centers, and warehousing. It encourages investment in value addition and supply chain management, creating employment and boosting the rural economy through agribusiness.

Watch Video Official Link

8. Bihar Udyan Yojana

This horticulture promotion scheme supports farmers who wish to grow high-value crops like turmeric, ginger, and seasonal vegetables. It also includes subsidies for rooftop and terrace gardening in urban and semi-urban areas. The scheme helps farmers shift toward more profitable and diversified agricultural practices, improving income and nutrition.

1. Mukhyamantri Khet Suraksha Yojana

Provides up to ₹1.43 lakh/ha (≈ 60% subsidy) for installing solar-powered electric fences (12 V + siren) to deter stray animals like nilgai, boars, and cattle. Initially introduced in Bundelkhand in July 2023 and with budget increased from ₹75 cr to ₹350 cr, it was expanded statewide. The scheme empowers farmers to protect crops without harming animals, and further funding (~₹50 cr) was allocated in Feb 2024 for rollout across forest-adjacent districts.

The scheme promotes water conservation, reduces dependency on borewells, and supports sustainable agriculture in arid and semi-arid zones of Rajasthan.
